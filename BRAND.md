## The Story

### The Problem

Israel has tens of thousands of vacation rental properties — צימרים, apartments, guest houses. Most are managed by individuals or small teams who are genuinely talented at hospitality. They know their guests. They care about the experience.

But the operations? They're held together with duct tape.

Separate calendar apps that don't sync. Payment tracking in Excel. Guest communication across three different WhatsApp threads. Cleaning schedules texted manually. No visibility into whether the business is actually profitable.

They're spending 20+ hours a week on operational drag — time that should go toward growth, service, or simply rest.

### The Insight

The problem isn't people. The problem is infrastructure.

The largest property management companies have custom software, dedicated operations teams, automation pipelines. Individual operators have Excel.

**That gap is the Atlas opportunity.**

### The Solution

Atlas is the operating system for the modern Israeli property manager. It centralizes every workflow — bookings, guests, cleaning, payments, communication, revenue intelligence — into a single, intelligent platform.

Not just a tool. An operating layer. Infrastructure that runs your business in the background while you focus on what matters.

**The result:** Property managers using Atlas save 20+ hours per week, eliminate double-bookings entirely, and finally have clear visibility into the financial health of their business.

## Brand Personality — 5 Pillars

### 1. CONFIDENT
Atlas knows its domain deeply. It speaks with quiet authority — never boastful, never uncertain. It doesn't need to oversell because the product speaks clearly.

*In copy:* "חוסך 20+ שעות שבועיות." Not "עשוי לחסוך זמן מסוים."

### 2. DIRECT
Israeli directness (ישרות) is not a flaw — it's a feature. Atlas doesn't hide behind corporate language. It says what it means, clearly and immediately.

*In copy:* "אפס הזמנות כפולות." Not "מצמצם משמעותית את הסיכון להזמנות כפולות."

### 3. WARM
Atlas is technology that cares. It celebrates your wins (toast messages that feel human). It delivers bad news with kindness. It speaks to you as a person running a business, not as a "user" in a system.

*In copy:* "ברכות! ההזמנה אושרה." Not "פעולה הושלמה בהצלחה."

### 4. INTELLIGENT
Atlas thinks ahead. It notices patterns. It suggests actions before you think to ask. Intelligence isn't a feature — it's the personality of the entire product.

*In UX:* Revenue Intelligence flags a pricing opportunity. The AI assistant anticipates follow-up questions. Automations trigger before you remember to trigger them.

### 5. RELIABLE
Like the Titan, Atlas never drops the ball. It is the system you trust when something matters. Uptime, data integrity, accurate reporting — these aren't product specs, they're brand promises.

*In copy:* SLA commitments, transparent status page, proactive incident communication.

## Voice & Tone

### Core Principles

**Lead with benefits, not features**
> "חוסך 20+ שעות שבועיות" → not "מרכזת את כל תהליכי העבודה"

**Use concrete numbers**

## Color System

### Primary Palette

| Token | Hex | Name | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| `--brand-teal` | `#00D1C1` | **Atlas Teal** | Primary brand, CTAs, links, focus rings |
| `--brand-teal-glow` | `#00EEDD` | **Atlas Glow** | Hover states, highlights |
| `--brand-teal-deep` | `#00A89A` | **Atlas Deep** | Active/pressed states |
| `--brand-teal-dark` | `#007A75` | **Atlas Dark Teal** | Strong accents, dark mode primary |
| `--brand-night` | `#0B1220` | **Atlas Night** | Primary text, dark surfaces |
| `--brand-night-surface` | `#111827` | **Atlas Night Surface** | Card bg in dark contexts |

**About Atlas Teal:** The color of the Mediterranean Sea meeting the sky. Energetic, modern, distinctly Israeli. It communicates trust without being cold, vibrancy without being aggressive. It is immediately recognizable as Atlas.

**About Atlas Night:** Deep navy, like the sky the Titan holds. Premium, authoritative, focused. It anchors the brand and prevents Teal from feeling lightweight.

### Neutral Palette

| Token | Hex | Name | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| `--brand-sand` | `#F2E9DB` | **Atlas Sand** | Section backgrounds, warm dividers |
| `--brand-sand-light` | `#FAF5EE` | **Atlas Sand Light** | Softer section backgrounds |
| `--page-bg` | `#F4F6FB` | **Atlas Cloud** | App page background |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | **Atlas White** | Card surfaces, modals |
| `--atlas-border-soft` | `rgba(15,23,42,0.07)` | **Atlas Mist** | Borders, dividers |

### Accent Palette

| Token | Hex | Name | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| `--brand-gold` | `#F5A623` | **Atlas Gold** | Revenue, success, premium tier |
| `--brand-coral` | `#FF6B6B` | **Atlas Coral** | Alerts, cancellations, urgent warnings |
| `--brand-indigo` | `#6C63FF` | **Atlas Indigo** | AI features, automations, intelligent actions |

### Usage Rules

1. **Teal is the hero.** It should dominate interactive elements (buttons, links, focus states).
2. **Night anchors.** Primary text is always Night or near-black. Never grey primary text.
3. **Gold = money.** Use exclusively for revenue metrics, payment confirmations, premium tier.
4. **Coral = urgent.** Reserve for truly critical states: cancellations, errors, overdue payments.
5. **Indigo = intelligence.** Exclusive to AI assistant, automations, smart suggestions.
6. **Sand = breathing room.** Use for section separators, landing page breaks — never in the app dashboard.

## Typography

### Font Stack

| Role | Font | Weights | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary (LTR)** | Heebo | 400, 500, 600, 700, 800, 900 | All UI text |
| **Primary (RTL)** | Rubik | 400, 500, 600, 700 | Hebrew-first contexts |
| **Monospace** | JetBrains Mono | 500, 600, 700 | Numbers, financial data, tables |

**Why Heebo?** Heebo is designed specifically for Hebrew text while maintaining elegance in Latin characters. It carries warmth without sacrificing professionalism. The weights from 400–900 give us a complete expressive range from body copy to impact headlines.

### Type Scale

| Token | Size | Weight | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Display | 3.75rem (60px) | 900 | Hero headlines only |
| H1 | 3rem (48px) | 800 | Page hero, landing sections |
| H2 | 2.25rem (36px) | 700 | Section headings |
| H3 | 1.875rem (30px) | 700 | Sub-sections |
| H4 | 1.5rem (24px) | 600 | Card titles, panel headers |
| H5 | 1.25rem (20px) | 600 | Sub-headings |
| Large Body | 1.125rem (18px) | 400 | Introduction paragraphs |
| Body | 1rem (16px) | 400 | Standard body copy |
| Small | 0.875rem (14px) | 400 | Secondary text, table cells |
| XS | 0.75rem (12px) | 500 | Labels, captions, badges |

### Typography Rules

1. **Tight tracking on large text.** Headings above 24px use `letter-spacing: -0.025em`.
2. **Tabular numbers everywhere financial.** All monetary values use JetBrains Mono with `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums`.
3. **Never center-align body copy** in RTL contexts. Left-align (which is right-align in RTL) for readability.
4. **Line height scales inversely with size.** Display: 1.1. Body: 1.5. Never fight this.

## Logo

### The Mark

The Atlas logotype uses **Heebo Black (900)** for maximum impact. The letterforms are set tight (`tracking: -0.04em`) for density and confidence.

**Full Color:** Atlas Teal (`#00D1C1`) wordmark on white or dark backgrounds.
**Reversed:** White wordmark on Atlas Night (`#0B1220`).
**Monochrome:** Atlas Night only, no teal — for embossed/single-color contexts.

### Clear Space

Always maintain clear space equal to the cap-height of the "A" letterform on all four sides. Never crowd the logo.

### Minimum Size

Digital: 24px height. Print: 10mm height. Below these sizes, use the icon-only mark.

### Don'ts

- Never stretch or distort proportions
- Never place on a busy background without a clean zone
- Never recolor — only the official color variants above
- Never add drop shadows to the wordmark
- Never use a font other than Heebo Black for the logotype

## Motion System

### Design Principle

**Motion carries meaning. Every transition communicates state.**

Atlas animations are purposeful, fast, and spring-based. They make the interface feel alive and responsive without being distracting. If an animation doesn't communicate something useful, remove it.

### Easing Curves

| Curve | Token | CSS | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Punchy Out** | `--atlas-ease-out` | `cubic-bezier(0.23, 1, 0.32, 1)` | Elements entering the screen |
| **Fast Exit** | (inline) | `cubic-bezier(0.55, 0, 1, 0.45)` | Elements leaving the screen |
| **Smooth** | `--atlas-ease-in-out` | `cubic-bezier(0.77, 0, 0.175, 1)` | State transitions, toggles |
| **Spring** | `--atlas-ease-spring` | `cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.3, 0.64, 1)` | Interactive elements, CTAs |

### Duration Scale

| Duration | Token | Value | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instant | `--duration-instant` | 80ms | Hover bg color changes |
| Fast | `--duration-fast` | 150ms | Icon swaps, badge changes |
| Normal | `--duration-normal` | 250ms | State changes, toggles |
| Enter | `--atlas-duration-enter` | 420ms | Page and panel enters |
| Slow | `--duration-slow` | 600ms | Large reveals, hero elements |
| Scenic | `--duration-scenic` | 800ms | Landing page animations |

### Stagger Pattern

Lists, grids, and dashboards stagger at **80ms intervals** (`--atlas-stagger-step`). This creates a sense of visual narrative — information arrives in sequence, guiding the eye.

### Rules

1. **Enter fast, exit faster.** Entering elements take the full enter duration. Exiting elements should leave in ~60% of the time.
2. **No animation on scroll events on mobile.** Only trigger stagger on page load, not every scroll event.
3. **Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.** All animations must disable gracefully.
4. **Hover states max 150ms.** Background color changes on hover should never exceed 150ms.

## Shadows & Depth

Atlas uses a three-layer depth system:

| Layer | Token | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Flat** | `--shadow-xs` | `0 1px 2px rgba(15,23,42,0.04)` — Inline cards, input fields |
| **Raised** | `--shadow-sm` | Standard cards, dropdowns |
| **Float** | `--shadow-md` | Modals, active panels |
| **Overlay** | `--shadow-lg` | Full overlays, command palette |
| **Billboard** | `--shadow-xl` | Hero elements, landing page |

**Teal Glow:** Interactive CTAs and focused elements use `--shadow-teal-md` to reinforce the brand color in interactive states.

**Rule:** Shadows go deeper as elements come closer to the user. Never use heavy shadows on flat, background-layer elements.

## Iconography

**Library:** Lucide React (consistent with existing codebase).

**Sizing:** Always use the standard 16/18/20/24px sizes. Never scale icons oddly.

**Stroke weight:** Default (1.5px) for UI icons. Use 2px weight for emphasis (empty states, section headers).

**Color rule:**
- UI icons take `currentColor` — they inherit text color naturally
- Decorative/featured icons use the icon container system (`.icon-teal`, `.icon-gold`, `.icon-indigo`, etc.)

## Layout & Grid

**Max content width:** 1280px (`xl` breakpoint)

**Page padding:** 
- Desktop: 24px horizontal
- Tablet: 20px horizontal  
- Mobile: 16px horizontal

**Grid system:** 12-column, 24px gap (Tailwind `gap-6`)

**Card grid:** 3-column on desktop → 2-column on tablet → 1-column on mobile

**Dashboard density:** Optimized for information density at 1280px+. Avoid excessive whitespace in the app shell — content should feel productive, not sparse.

## Dos and Don'ts: Visual

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| Use Teal as the single primary brand color | Use multiple competing colors for primary actions |
| Maintain the Teal–Night–White three-tone foundation | Mix warm and cool greys in the same view |
| Use Gold exclusively for financial/revenue metrics | Use Gold as a generic "success" color |
| Keep the brand color on scroll thumb (teal) | Change scrollbar color to grey |
| Animate lists with 80ms stagger | Animate all elements simultaneously |
| Use sand/warm backgrounds on landing pages | Use sand inside the app dashboard |
| Keep cards clean with `--shadow-sm` | Stack multiple box-shadow effects on a single element |
